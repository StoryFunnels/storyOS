## Why
Transactional emails are currently a single line of plain text (`You've been invited…
Accept: <url>`). They look broken/spammy — no greeting, no branding, a raw URL instead of
a button. Now that delivery works (Resend + verified sender), the emails need to look like
they come from a real product.

## What
- Add a small branded HTML email layout: StoryOS wordmark/logo, cream/navy brand colors,
  a greeting, body copy, a proper **button** CTA, and a footer (from-name, unsubscribe/help
  line). Keep a plain-text fallback part (multipart) for deliverability.
- Extend `sendMail` to accept `html` alongside `text` (nodemailer supports both); render
  through a tiny template helper — no heavy MJML dependency needed for v1.
- Convert the existing sends to templates:
  - **Workspace invite** — "You're invited to {workspace}", role, "Accept invite" button.
  - **Email verification** — "Confirm your email" button.
  - **Password reset** — "Reset your password" button.
- Use table-based, inline-styled HTML (email-client safe); test in Gmail + Apple Mail.

## Acceptance
- [ ] Invite/verification/reset emails render as branded HTML with a greeting + button.
- [ ] Plain-text fallback present (multipart/alternative).
- [ ] Links use `WEB_URL`; renders correctly in Gmail and Apple Mail.
- [ ] `MAIL_FROM` display name is "StoryOS".
